What it's all about

whence come the thousands of photographs used every month by newpapers and magazines?

morethan that, whence do the photographs come which are used by makers of calendars, postcards, for advertisenents, and for ittustrating books, stories?

This Little book is a practical, up-to-the-minute answer to the question: "How can I make my canera-work profitable?

A. H. Beardsley, publisher, photo-Era Magazine.

At first thought, the answer 1s, "From professional photographers and publisher-photo-services." But professional photographers do not produce one-third of the photographs used, and publisher-photo-services are supplied by that same large mumber of camerists that supply publications with most of their prints.

No one can deny that the greatest number of prints published are bought from amateur photographers in towns no larger than the average, and sometimes smaller

The camerist does not have to get in an air-ship and fly to Africa in order to produce photographs that will sell. Read what waldon Faucett says, himself a success at selling his photographers

The photographer is apt to think that all his ambitions would be realised if only he could journey to foreign shores or to distant corners of our country; or if he could attend the spectacular events that focus the attention of the world now and then. This is a delusion. The real triumph is thet of the photographer who utilizes the material ready at hand in his own district, be it large or small.

And more, a person does not have to be an expert photographer in order to succeed at the work, Here is what one prominent writer says about It

The requirements of the field are well within the capabilities of even the beginner in photography, viz.; the ability to make good negatives and good prints, the ability to recognise news-value, and a methodical plan to find the market where the prints will find acceptance. The man or woman who can seet these requirements should be fairly successful fron the beginning, and will open up quickly new avenues of special work and profit."

In short, ability to make metaphors, create lovely heroines or such is mat at all necessary to the successful selling of photographs to publications

is the field overcrowded? No,if there were ten times as many persons engaged in the work they could all keep themselves busy.

The field-how wide is it? Get out your map of the world. The field for making photographs from the top margin to the bottom, and from the left to the right, The tield for selling photographs-which is more to the point-extends over about five thousand publications which use prints; not to speak of a few score of other markets.
